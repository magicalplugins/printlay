When a DTF transfer won't stick — or lifts after a wash — it's almost always one of a handful of process issues, not the artwork. Work through this checklist.

## 1. Heat, time and pressure

The big three at the press:

- **Temperature** — too low and the adhesive never fully activates. Check the platen actually reaches the set temp.
- **Dwell time** — hold for the full recommended time; don't rush it.
- **Pressure** — firm and even. Uneven pressure (seams, zips, platen wear) leaves patches that don't bond.

## 2. Powder application and curing

- **Too little powder** — weak bond. **Too much** — stiff, cracky feel.
- **Uneven powder** — patchy adhesion.
- **Under-cured powder** — the most common cause of wash-off. The powder must fully melt during curing, not just warm up.

## 3. Peel timing

Films are **hot peel** or **cold peel**. Peeling at the wrong moment lifts the design or distorts it. Follow your film's spec.

## 4. Garment and prep

- **Pre-press** the garment to remove moisture and creases.
- **Fabric type** — some coatings and waterproof finishes resist bonding.

## 5. The file (occasionally)

Rarely the artwork, but worth a glance: a missing or weak **white underbase** can make colours look washed out on dark fabric. See the [print-ready PDF checklist](/blog/print-ready-pdf-checklist).

## Do a wash test

Before committing a big order, press one transfer and wash it a few times. It's the fastest way to catch curing and adhesion problems before they cost you a reprint — and reprints, like [film waste](/blog/reduce-dtf-film-waste), eat your margin.
