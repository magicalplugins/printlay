Building a gang sheet by hand isn't complicated — it's just fiddly and slow. Here's the exact process, whether you do it manually or let software handle it.

## 1. Set your sheet size

Create a canvas at your **printer or roll width** — most commonly 22 inches (≈56 cm) wide for DTF — by the length you need. For UV DTF, work to your bed size (often 12" × 12" or A3). See the [gang sheet size guide](/blog/gang-sheet-size-guide) for the standards worth sticking to.

## 2. Bring in your designs at the right resolution

Place each design at **300 DPI at final print size**. Resizing a small image up to fill a sheet is the most common cause of soft, pixelated transfers. Not sure if a file is sharp enough? Check it with the [bleed & DPI calculator](/tools/bleed-dpi-calculator).

## 3. Nest them tightly

Arrange designs to fill the sheet with a **consistent gap** between them (around 3–5 mm for DTF). Rotate designs where it helps them tile. Mixing sizes to fill gaps — true nesting — beats a rigid grid every time. Use the [gang sheet calculator](/tools/gang-sheet-calculator) to see how many fit before you start.

## 4. Add bleed and cut lines (stickers)

For stickers and UV DTF decals, extend artwork **2–3 mm past the cut**, then add a contour line on the **CutContour** spot colour so the cutter knows where to cut. Full detail in the [sticker cut lines guide](/guides/sticker-cut-lines).

## 5. Export print-ready

Export a **PDF** with the correct dimensions, embedded artwork, bleed, and the cut contour as a spot colour your RIP recognises. Double-check against the [print-ready PDF checklist](/blog/print-ready-pdf-checklist).
