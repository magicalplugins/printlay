UV DTF gang sheets follow the same logic as any gang sheet, but the small decal sizes and the AB-film workflow add a few specifics. Here's the process.

## 1. Set the sheet to your bed or roll

Create your canvas at your UV DTF size — commonly **12" × 12"**, **A3** or **A4**. Don't exceed your printable area, and leave a small margin at the edges.

## 2. Size and place your decals

UV DTF decals are usually small — logos, badges, small graphics. Place each at **300 DPI at final size**; these go on hard goods viewed up close, so resolution shows. Check files with the [bleed & DPI calculator](/tools/bleed-dpi-calculator).

## 3. Nest aggressively

Because decals are small and varied, **nest to fill the gaps** rather than using a fixed grid. Keep a consistent ~3 mm gap so the cutter has room and the laminate weeds cleanly. The [gang sheet calculator](/tools/gang-sheet-calculator) shows how many decals fit your sheet.

## 4. Add bleed and kiss-cut contours

Extend artwork **2–3 mm past the cut** so there's no white edge after application, then add a **kiss-cut contour** around each decal. A [sticker cut line generator](/features/sticker-cut-line-generator) traces these automatically — see the full [cut lines guide](/guides/sticker-cut-lines) for the why.

## 5. Account for laminate and export

If you laminate, make sure the cut settings suit the laminated thickness. Export a **print-and-cut ready file** with the contour on a recognised spot colour (e.g. CutContour).
