Most reprints are avoidable. They come from the same handful of file problems — and a 60-second pre-flight catches nearly all of them. Run this before committing any sheet to the printer.

## The checklist

**1. Size** — the document is the **exact final size** (or your gang sheet size). No stray artboards or hidden objects.

**2. Bleed** — artwork extends **2–3 mm past** every trim/cut edge. Use the [bleed & DPI calculator](/tools/bleed-dpi-calculator) if unsure.

**3. Resolution** — every image is **300 DPI at final size**. Watch for small images scaled up — the classic soft-print cause.

**4. Colour** — the right colour space for your workflow, and (for DTF) a correct **white underbase** layer where needed.

**5. Cut lines** — for stickers/UV DTF, the contour is a **100% CutContour spot colour**, set to **overprint**, not a normal CMYK colour. See [sticker cut lines](/guides/sticker-cut-lines).

**6. Fonts** — outlined or fully embedded so nothing reflows or substitutes.

**7. Transparency & effects** — flattened where your RIP needs it; check drop shadows and blends didn't leave artefacts.

**8. Spacing** — on a gang sheet, consistent gaps so designs cut apart cleanly.
