"Best" depends on your shop — but the way you choose is the same. Here are the real options for building gang sheets in 2026 and how to weigh them.

## The three kinds of option

### 1. Doing it by hand (Illustrator / Photoshop)
The default starting point. Total control, no extra cost if you already own the software — but **slow, manual and error-prone**. You size the canvas, import each design, nudge spacing, draw cut lines and export, every single time. Fine for the occasional sheet; painful at volume. See [how to make a gang sheet](/blog/how-to-make-a-gang-sheet) for what that involves.

### 2. Supplier / storefront builders
Many DTF film suppliers offer a free online builder — convenient, but usually **tied to buying from that supplier** and limited in layout control, formats and export. Great if you outsource printing; restrictive if you print your own and want to own the workflow.

### 3. Dedicated gang sheet software
Purpose-built tools that **nest automatically, add cut lines, and export print-ready files** independent of any supplier. The best fit for shops printing their own sheets at volume.

## What to look for

- **Tight automatic nesting** — the core job; it drives your cost per print.
- **Format support** — DTF, UV DTF and stickers, with the right sizes and spacing for each.
- **Cut lines** — automatic contours on a recognised spot colour (e.g. CutContour).
- **Print-ready export** — clean PDFs your RIP accepts, with bleed.
- **Vendor independence** — not locked to one supplier's storefront.
- **A catalogue** — reuse saved designs instead of re-importing.
- **Speed** — seconds per sheet, not half an hour.
