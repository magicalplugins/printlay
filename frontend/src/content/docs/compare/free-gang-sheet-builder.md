Search for a "free gang sheet builder" and you'll find plenty — almost all from **DTF suppliers**. They're genuinely useful, but it pays to understand *why* they're free before you build your workflow around one.

## Why most free builders exist

A supplier's free builder is really a **storefront**. You arrange your designs, and the only thing you can do with the finished sheet is **order it printed by that supplier**. That's a fair trade if you outsource printing — but it comes with a catch.

## The catch: supplier lock-in

- You can build a sheet, but you **can't take it elsewhere** or print it in-house.
- Switch suppliers and you start over in their tool.
- Sizes, exports and cut-line options are whatever the supplier allows.

If you **print your own sheets**, or use **more than one supplier**, a locked builder works against you.

## What to look for instead

Whether free or paid, a good gang sheet builder should:

- **Export a print-ready file** (PDF/PNG/TIFF) you can send to **any** RIP.
- **Nest designs tightly** and keep [consistent gaps](/blog/how-to-make-a-gang-sheet).
- **Generate [cut lines](/guides/sticker-cut-lines)** for stickers.
- Support your formats — **DTF, [UV DTF](/guides/uv-dtf-gang-sheets), stickers**.
- Not force you (or your customers) through one vendor's checkout.

| | Supplier free builder | Builder you control |

## The takeaway

"Free" usually means "free if you print with us." That's fine if you outsource to one supplier — but if you print in-house or want flexibility, choose a builder that **exports a print-ready file you own** and isn't tied to anyone's storefront.
