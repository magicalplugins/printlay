**CMYK** is the four-colour model used in printing: **C**yan, **M**agenta, **Y**ellow and **K**ey (black). Inks are layered in these four channels to reproduce colour on paper and film.

It differs from **RGB**, the additive model screens use. Because RGB can show colours CMYK can't print, designs are often converted to CMYK (or a printer profile) before output so what you see is closer to what prints. [DTF](/glossary/dtf) adds a white channel on top of CMYK for printing on dark materials.
