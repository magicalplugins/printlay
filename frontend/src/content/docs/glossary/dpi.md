**DPI** (dots per inch) measures print resolution — how many dots of ink fit in an inch. **300 DPI at final print size** is the standard for sharp results. For large prints viewed at a distance, 150 DPI can be acceptable; below ~150 DPI, artwork starts to look soft or pixelated.

The common mistake is scaling a small image up to fill a sheet, which drops its effective DPI. Check any file with the [bleed & DPI calculator](/tools/bleed-dpi-calculator).
