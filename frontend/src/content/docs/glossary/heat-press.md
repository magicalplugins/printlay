A **heat press** is a machine that applies **controlled heat, time and pressure** to bond a transfer onto a garment or item. It's the final application step for [DTF](/glossary/dtf), [HTV](/glossary/htv) and screen-print transfers.

The three settings — **temperature, dwell time and pressure** — must match the transfer's spec. Getting any of them wrong is the most common cause of transfers that don't stick or peel after washing; see [DTF transfer troubleshooting](/blog/dtf-transfer-troubleshooting).
