**PET film** (polyethylene terephthalate) is the **transfer film** that [DTF](/glossary/dtf) designs are printed onto. After printing, it's coated with adhesive powder, cured, then heat-pressed onto fabric, and finally peeled away — leaving the design behind.

It comes in **hot-peel** and **cold-peel** types, which determine when you peel the film after pressing. Because a DTF printer lays ink across the full film width regardless of use, packing designs tightly on the film matters — see [reduce DTF film waste](/blog/reduce-dtf-film-waste).
