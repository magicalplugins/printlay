**Plastisol** is a PVC-based ink widely used in **screen printing** and **screen print transfers**. It doesn't dry by evaporation — it must be **cured with heat** to fuse, producing a thick, opaque, hard-wearing layer of colour.

Because plastisol sits on top of the fabric and is separated by colour, it's best for bold, limited-colour designs. For full-colour or short-run work, a digitally printed transfer like [DTF](/glossary/dtf) is often more practical — see [screen print transfers vs DTF](/blog/screen-print-transfers-vs-dtf).
