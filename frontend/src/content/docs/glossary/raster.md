A **raster** image is made of a grid of **pixels** — PNG, JPEG and TIFF are all raster formats. Its quality depends on **resolution** ([DPI](/glossary/dpi)): enough pixels at print size look sharp, too few look soft or pixelated, and enlarging a small raster doesn't add real detail.

Photographs and full-colour designs are raster. For shapes a cutter must follow, you need [vector](/glossary/vector) paths instead. For DTF, aim for 300 DPI raster artwork at final print size — see [best file format for DTF](/blog/best-file-format-for-dtf).
