**Sublimation** is a printing method where heat turns solid ink directly into gas, which then bonds into a **light polyester or poly-coated** surface — dyeing the material rather than sitting on top of it. There's no white ink, so it only works on light, polyester-rich substrates (mugs, coasters, light garments).

Designs are printed onto **sublimation paper**, then heat-pressed. Like [DTF](/glossary/dtf), sublimation prints can be [ganged up](/blog/sublimation-gang-sheets) to save paper and ink.
