A **white underbase** is a layer of white ink printed beneath the colour channels so the design stays **vivid and opaque** on dark or coloured materials. Without it, colours printed on a dark garment look dull and see-through.

In [DTF](/glossary/dtf), white is a separate ink channel and usually the most expensive consumable — managing it well is key to both print quality and cost. A missing or weak underbase is a common cause of washed-out prints; see [DTF troubleshooting](/blog/dtf-transfer-troubleshooting).
