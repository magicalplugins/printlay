**UV DTF** is the fastest-growing corner of custom printing — and it's where good **UV print layout** pays off most. Because UV DTF decals are usually small and go onto hard goods, packing them efficiently onto a gang sheet has an outsized effect on your cost and your throughput.

## What makes UV DTF different

Unlike standard DTF (which presses onto fabric with heat), UV DTF produces **stickers/decals for hard surfaces** — tumblers, bottles, glass, phone cases, signage. The workflow is different in three important ways:

- **AB film, not PET.** UV DTF uses a two-layer film: the **A** film carries the printed, UV-cured, laminated design; the **B** film transfers it onto the product.
- **No heat or water.** You peel, position, burnish and remove the transfer film. That's it.
- **Cut lines matter.** Each decal needs a contour so it can be separated and applied on its own.

For a side-by-side, see [DTF vs UV DTF](/blog/dtf-vs-uv-dtf).

## UV print layout: the essentials

A clean UV DTF gang sheet comes down to five decisions:

### 1. Sheet size
Match the sheet to your printer bed or roll. Common UV DTF sizes are **12" × 12"**, **A3** and **A4**. Standardise on one or two sizes so your layouts and pricing stay predictable.

### 2. Decal size and spacing
UV DTF designs are usually small (logos, badges, small graphics). Keep a consistent gap — around **3 mm** — so the cutter has room and decals weed cleanly without tearing the laminate.

### 3. Nesting
Because decals are small and varied, **true nesting** (mixing sizes to fill gaps) beats a rigid grid. The tighter you nest, the more decals per sheet and the lower your cost each.

### 4. Cut / kiss-cut lines
Every decal needs a contour line. A [sticker cut line generator](/features/sticker-cut-line-generator) traces the artwork automatically so you don't draw paths by hand.

### 5. Laminate and bleed
Extend artwork slightly past the cut line (bleed) so the laminated edge is clean with no white halo, and account for the laminate layer in your layout.

## Costing UV DTF sheets

Because decals are small, you can often fit a lot per sheet — which means your per-decal cost can be very low if you pack well, or wasteful if you don't. Run your decal and sheet sizes through the [gang sheet calculator](/tools/gang-sheet-calculator) to see fit, sheets needed and cost per decal.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- **Gaps too tight** — decals tear when weeding the laminate.
- **No bleed** — white edges show after application.
- **Inconsistent sizes** — wrecks nesting and pricing.
- **Low-resolution art** — UV DTF is viewed up close on hard goods, so 300 DPI matters.

## The bottom line

UV DTF rewards tight, consistent layout more than almost any other print format because the decals are small and the volumes are high. Standardise your sheet sizes, automate your cut lines, and nest aggressively — and UV DTF becomes one of the most profitable things your shop prints.
